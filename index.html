About Me

Hi There! I am Shivansh, currently working as a Senior Developer at UST Innovation Labs. My interests lie mainly in autonomous mobile robots, drones, and prosthesis development. I am also particulary passionate about interfacing AI and LLM's into robotics. I've participated in several national and international competitions and was the former head of The Robotics Club at IIT Palakkad.

I have done various projects in robotics, utilizing both ROS and ROS 2, and have integrated AI into various systems. I also have extensive experience interfacing robots with various simulation environments, including Gazebo-classic, Ignition, Isaac Sim, and Isaac Lab. Additionally, I have developed 3D models and robotic systems using Fusion 360 and SolidWorks, and have worked with a wide range of sensors for autonomous development. 3-Fingered Prosthetic arm

Design and Development of a Cost-effective, 3-Fingered Prosthetic arm

  • Designed a cost-effective, lightweight prosthetic arm for transradial amputees using 3D printing and readily available components.
  • Achieved fine motor control in the prosthetic, enabling key, spherical, cylindrical, and pinch grips.
  • Research paper accepted at ICRAME(International Conference on Recent Advancements in Mechanical Engineering, 2023), to be published by Springer as a proceedings book volume.

Pacifista

A multi-terrain, multipurpose robot.

  • Designed and constructed the "Pacifista" robot, capable of navigating diverse terrains including rough landscapes and stairs, and integrated LLM for reasoning.
  • Engineered advanced features like autonomous terrain mapping using Frontier Exploration to enhance autonomous operational capabilities.
  • Integrated LLM by using huggingface transformers library to finetune quantized Vicuna 13B model, for reasoning capabilities.
  • Developed transformable wheels for the robot, which adjust from a circular wheeled configuration to a spoked configuration to accommodate both smooth and rough terrains. Wheel design currently awaiting patent approval.

Autonomous Mecanum Robot in Isaac Sim

Simulation and control of a four wheeled mecanum robot in Isaac Sim
